110-minute revision sequence

Double revision lesson

A two-period lesson for diagnosing skills, rebuilding methods, and finishing with focused mixed practice.

2 x 55 minutes Lesson 1: diagnose and rebuild Lesson 2: apply and consolidate

Teacher flow

Lesson 1: diagnose and rebuild

Start broad, then let students target the topic areas that need the most immediate repair.

0-8 min

Readiness check

  1. State the domain of f(x)=x+3.
  2. Solve x2-5x+6=0.
  3. Find the period of y=3sin(2x).
  4. Find Pr(A∩B) when Pr(A)=0.6 and Pr(B|A)=0.25.
8-18 min

Traffic-light marking

  • Green: correct method and correct answer.
  • Amber: correct idea but incomplete notation or arithmetic error.
  • Red: unsure how to start.
  • Students choose two red or amber topics for the station block.
18-38 min

Skill stations

FunctionsDomain, range, inverse notation.
TrigExact values, periods, transformed graphs.
QuadraticsFactors, discriminants, turning points.
TransformationsReflections, dilations, translations.
ProbabilityTree diagrams, conditional probability, complements.
Coordinate geometryGradients, midpoints, distances, line rules.
38-50 min

Error hunt

Example 1: inverse function

A common error is to swap x and y but forget to swap the domain and range. Correct the rule, then state the new domain.

Example 2: trigonometric period

A common error is to multiply the period by the coefficient inside the bracket. For sine and cosine, use 2π|n|.

50-55 min

Reset checkpoint

Students write one sentence: "The topic I need to stabilise before mixed practice is..."

Second period

Lesson 2: apply and consolidate

Move from targeted repair into timed, mixed, clearly communicated mathematical work.

0-5 min

Re-entry warm-up

Students answer one question from their checkpoint topic. Keep it short enough to mark immediately.

5-25 min

Timed mixed practice

  1. Find the maximal domain and range of a square-root function after a translation.
  2. Solve a quadratic equation and interpret the discriminant.
  3. Sketch one transformed circular function over a restricted interval.
  4. Use a two-stage tree diagram to calculate a conditional probability.
  5. Find the equation of a line through two given points, then use it in a short coordinate-geometry argument.
25-38 min

Pair explanation

  • Choose one response from timed practice.
  • Explain the first line, the key method step, and the final answer.
  • Partner checks notation, domain restrictions, and graph labels.
38-48 min

Independent correction

Students use topic cards, drills, and solutions to improve one incomplete response. The corrected version should show the method, not just the answer.

48-55 min

Exit ticket

  1. Solve one exact-answer algebra or trigonometry task.
  2. State the domain and range from one function or graph.
  3. Write the first revision task you will complete at home.
