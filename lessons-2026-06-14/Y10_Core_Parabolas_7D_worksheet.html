Learning intentions.
  • Use completing the square to write any quadratic in turning point form $y=(x-h)^2+k$
  • Read the turning point $(h,k)$ and axis of symmetry; find $x$- and $y$-intercepts
  • Sketch the parabola with key features labelled

Key results

To complete the square on $x^2+bx$, add and subtract $\left(\tfrac{b}{2}\right)^2$.  In $y=(x-h)^2+k$: turning point $(h,k)$, axis of symmetry $x=h$.  $y$-intercept: put $x=0$.   $x$-intercepts: put $y=0$ and take $\pm\sqrt{\ }$ of both sides.
