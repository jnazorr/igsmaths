Learning intentions.
  • Count total equally-likely outcomes for an arrangement or selection
  • Count favourable outcomes using restrictions, "exactly" and "at least"
  • Write each probability as an exact, simplified fraction

Key results

For equally-likely outcomes $P(\text{event})=\dfrac{n(\text{favourable})}{n(\text{total})}$.  Count both with the multiplication principle, $^nP_r$ or $^nC_r$, then simplify.  "At least one" is often quickest as $1-P(\text{none})$.
